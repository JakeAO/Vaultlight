# Vaultlight Campaign Overview

## Campaign Structure

### Act I: Assembly & Reconnaissance
**Sessions 0-3**
- Crew assembly and initial briefing
- Research and scouting phase
- Equipment acquisition
- First complications arise

**Key Themes:** Trust, preparation, the allure of the impossible

### Act II: The Approach
**Sessions 4-7**
- Infiltration of the Obsidian Quarter
- Overcoming outer defenses
- Discovery that the vault is not what it seems
- Encounters with other interested parties

**Key Themes:** Paranoia, competing interests, cosmic unease

### Act III: The Descent
**Sessions 8-11**
- Breaching the vault's inner layers
- Revelations about the city's true nature
- Sanity and reality become questionable
- The artifact's true purpose revealed

**Key Themes:** Cosmic horror, sacrifice, existential dread

### Act IV: Consequences
**Sessions 12-15**
- Escape (or transformation)
- Dealing with what was unleashed
- Betrayals and revelations
- Final confrontation with the truth

**Key Themes:** Corruption, choice, the price of knowledge

## Major Factions

### The Keepers (Hidden)
Descendants of those who created the Twilight as protection. They've been watching the vault for generations.

### The Awakened (Cultists)
Believe the Twilight should end, regardless of what comes after. The "client" is one of them.

### The Umbral Syndicate
Genuinely unaware of the vault's true nature but seeking ancient artifacts for profit.

### The City Authority
Know more than they admit but are fractured in their response.

## Key NPCs - Secret Motivations

### Marcus Vale
One of the Keepers, testing whether the crew is worthy of knowing the truth or if they'll trigger catastrophe.

### The Client
Maestra Nyx, leader of the Awakened, who believes freeing what lies beneath will bring "true night" and end the half-existence of the Twilight.

### Detective Winters
Former Keeper who lost faith, now trying to prevent anyone from reaching the vault through legal means.

## Improvisation Notes

- Let players discover clues at their own pace
- The horror should escalate gradually
- Every "success" should come with a cost or complication
- NPCs can shift allegiances based on player actions
- The Twilight itself may respond to the crew's actions

## Themes to Emphasize

- **Noir:** Moral ambiguity, everyone has secrets, nothing is as it seems
- **Heist:** Planning vs. improvisation, teamwork, specialists working together
- **Cosmic Horror:** Insignificance, forbidden knowledge, things beyond understanding
- **Choice:** The crew's decisions matter, even when all choices are bad
