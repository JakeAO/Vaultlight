Use these as quick reminders for what each skill covers in Vaultlight. Examples are tuned to the setting's rain-soaked, stalled-tech noir.

---

## Academics

Mundane education and book learning, including history, sciences, and medicine.

**Examples:** Stabilize a gunshot wound with a battered field manual; identify a discontinued circuit board in a dead terminal.

## Athletics

Physical movement, agility, and dodging.

**Examples:** Parkour across rain-slick rooftops; dive through closing subway doors.

## Burglary

Bypassing security, picking locks, and committing quiet crime.

**Examples:** Shim a magnetic lock with a homemade shunt; lift a wallet in a packed tram.

## Contacts

Knowing the right people and calling in favors.

**Examples:** Find a fence for hot goods; get a dock union tip about a late-night shipment.

## Crafts

Building, repairing, and improvising tech and gear.

**Examples:** Patch a sputtering getaway car; rig a signal jammer from scavenged parts.

## Deceive

Lying, bluffing, and false identities.

**Examples:** Pose as a city inspector to gain access; forge a delivery schedule on the fly.

## Drive

Handling vehicles under pressure and pulling maneuvers.

**Examples:** Thread a stolen sedan through narrow alleys; coax speed out of a tired engine.

## Empathy

Reading emotions and intentions.

**Examples:** Spot when an informant is terrified; sense a partner holding back vital info.

## Fight

Hand-to-hand combat and melee.

**Examples:** Knife fight in a foggy alley; brawl with a club bouncer.

## Investigate

Careful analysis and solving mysteries.

**Examples:** Reconstruct a break-in from scuffed floorboards; trace a paper trail in old ledgers.

## Lore

Specialized or arcane knowledge beyond Academics.

**Examples:** Recognize a warding sigil on a safe; recall rumors of a forgotten underpass cult.

## Notice

Immediate perception and alertness.

**Examples:** Spot a tail in a crowd; catch the soft click of a door latch in the rain.

## Physique

Strength, endurance, and toughness.

**Examples:** Force a jammed door; carry a wounded ally through an evacuation.

## Provoke

Intimidation and pushing reactions.

**Examples:** Rattle a guard into abandoning a post; needle a rival into a reckless move.

## Rapport

Building trust and friendly influence.

**Examples:** Win over a bartender for back-room access; smooth a crew argument before it erupts.

## Resources

Access to money, gear, or institutional backing.

**Examples:** Float a bribe on short notice; leverage a former employer to access a warehouse.

## Shoot

Ranged combat and marksmanship.

**Examples:** Pistol work in a tight hallway; a well-placed throwing knife to cut a rope.

## Stealth

Staying unseen or unheard.

**Examples:** Move through service tunnels without a sound; blend into a shift change at a factory gate.

## Will

Mental resilience and self-control.

**Examples:** Stay calm under interrogation; resist a whispering supernatural compulsion.
